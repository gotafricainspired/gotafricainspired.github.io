What's all this about? Is it a blog?

You might call it a blog, due to the format we use. But we prefer to see it as an independant publishing.

We are building and sharing a collection, actually several collections of media and articles related to african success stories or simply stories that can inspire all of us, african and non-african, in our lives, our work, our dreams.

We value authenticity, openness and sharing, and as you read through the posts, you should see these values reflected.

But I've already seen some of the stuff you publish, even years back

Short answer: The old can inspire the new.

Long answer: We are different from a classical news site, since we do not feature stories on the unique criteria that the topic is current, hot or trendy. Our timing is one of calmly looking at a timeframe of years and getting a 10ft look at things. We believe it is an interesting experience, and one that is needed as a complement and alternative to today's "get me the latest news on my phone from facebook" way.
We select great stories and creations from Africa and from the web, even if they were initially published several years ago, as long as they are inspirational and can contribute to a better world.

How do I use your site?

Basically, you can read the articles, see the videos, and listen to the audios posted, give feedback and share your own views by commenting.

But that's not all... You can also contribute.

I really like this. How can I contribute?

You can also take part to this collective publishing project.

  • As a blogger, journalist, or someone doing/building stuff, send us inspiring stories to feature. If you have original article, even better. It will be showcased in our "Guest posts" category.
  • As a designer or developer, help with building the site's features. Simply get in touch to propose your idea, or send pull requests on github.com.

Once your first contribution has been accepted, you become a member of the team.
